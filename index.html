Cookie Clicker in Scratch

A beginner-friendly step-by-step guide — no experience needed!

Steps: 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8 ~30 mins

What you'll build

Game goal
Click a cookie to earn points. Watch your score go up!
You'll learn
Sprites, costumes, variables, events, and loops
Time
About 20–30 minutes
Where to go
scratch.mit.edu → click Create
1
Open Scratch and set up your project
Creating a new project and removing the default cat
  1. Go to scratch.mit.edu in your browser and sign in (or create a free account).
  2. Click the orange Create button at the top of the page. A new project will open.
  3. You'll see a white stage on the right and a grey workspace on the left. There will be a cat sprite already there — we don't need it.
  4. In the sprite panel (bottom right), right-click the cat sprite and choose Delete. The stage will go blank.
Tip If you don't see a right-click option, hover over the cat sprite in the panel — a small trash bin icon will appear in the top-right corner of the sprite thumbnail. Click it to delete.
2
Create the Cookie sprite
Drawing or choosing a cookie to click

We need a cookie on screen to click. We'll draw a simple one using Scratch's built-in paint editor.

  1. Look at the bottom-right corner of the screen. Find the blue circle with a white cat silhouette — that's the Choose a Sprite button. Hover over it.
  2. A small menu pops up with four icons. Click the Paint icon (the paintbrush).
  3. A new blank sprite appears, and the costume editor opens on the left side of the screen.
  4. In the costume editor toolbar, click the Circle tool (looks like an oval outline).
  5. Pick a brown colour from the colour palette at the bottom — something like a warm chestnut brown.
  6. Make sure the Fill option is selected (not just the outline). You can tell because the filled square icon is active at the top-left of the editor.
  7. Hold Shift and drag to draw a circle on the canvas. Make it nice and big — roughly the size of your thumbnail.
  8. Now pick a slightly darker brown colour. Use the Circle tool again to draw several small circles on top of the cookie — these are your chocolate chips! Make them small and scatter them around.
  9. Click the Sprite name field at the top of the sprite panel (it probably says "Sprite1") and rename it to Cookie.
Shortcut — use a built-in sprite instead If drawing isn't your thing: hover over the Choose a Sprite button and click the magnifying glass icon instead. Search for "ball" or "button" and pick something round. Then rename it "Cookie".
3
Add a "clicked" costume for the cookie
Making the cookie look pressed when you click it

We'll make the cookie shrink slightly when clicked to give visual feedback. The easiest way is to add a second, smaller costume.

  1. Make sure your Cookie sprite is selected in the sprite panel.
  2. Click the Costumes tab at the very top-left of the screen (next to "Code" and "Sounds").
  3. You'll see your cookie drawing listed as costume1. Right-click on it and choose Duplicate. A copy called costume2 appears.
  4. Click on costume2 to select it.
  5. Click the Select All button (or press Ctrl+A) to select everything in the costume.
  6. Drag one of the corner handles inward to make the costume slightly smaller — about 80% of the original size. This is your "pressed" version.
Naming your costumes Double-click on "costume1" in the costumes list and rename it normal. Rename "costume2" to pressed. This makes it much easier to refer to them in your code later.
4
Create the Score variable
Setting up a counter to track how many cookies you've clicked

A variable is a named box that stores a number. We'll use one called "Score" to count clicks.

  1. Click the Code tab to go back to the coding area.
  2. In the block palette on the left, click the dark orange Variables category.
  3. Click the Make a Variable button.
  4. A dialog box appears. Type Score as the variable name.
  5. Make sure "For all sprites" is selected, then click OK.
  6. You'll see a new block appear: an orange rounded block that says Score. You'll also notice a small "Score: 0" display appears on your stage — that's the live readout!
Where is the score display? Look at the white stage (top right). You should see a small box with "Score 0" in it. You can drag it to any corner you like — put it in the top-left or top-right so it doesn't cover the cookie.
5
Code the cookie click
Adding blocks so clicking the cookie increases the score

Now for the fun part — writing the code! Make sure the Cookie sprite is selected in the sprite panel.

  1. Click the Events category (yellow blocks) in the block palette.
  2. Drag a when this sprite clicked block onto the empty grey coding area.
  3. Click the Looks category (purple blocks).
  4. Drag switch costume to and snap it below the click event. Click the dropdown and select pressed.
  5. Click the Variables category (dark orange blocks).
  6. Drag change Score by 1 and snap it below the costume block.
  7. Click the Control category (light orange blocks).
  8. Drag a wait 0.1 seconds block and snap it next. Change the number to 0.1.
  9. Click Looks again. Drag another switch costume to block and snap it at the bottom. Set the dropdown to normal.
Still need one more block! Make sure you didn't forget instruction #9 above — add a final "switch costume to normal" block at the bottom. Without it, the cookie will stay "pressed" forever after the first click.
6
Reset the score at the start
Making the game start fresh every time you press the green flag

Right now, if you click the green flag to restart, the score carries on from where it left off. Let's fix that.

  1. Make sure the Cookie sprite is still selected.
  2. Click the Events category.
  3. Drag a when ⚑ clicked block onto the coding area. Place it to the right of your other blocks — it will be its own separate stack.
  4. Click the Variables category.
  5. Drag set Score to 0 and snap it below the green flag block.
  6. Click the Looks category.
  7. Drag switch costume to normal and snap it at the bottom — this makes sure the cookie starts in its normal (not pressed) state.
7
Add a background
Making the stage look like a proper game screen

Right now the stage is plain white. Let's add a simple background to make it feel more like a game.

  1. Look at the bottom-right of the screen. Below the sprite panel, there's a tiny stage icon labelled Stage. Click on it.
  2. Now look at the bottom-right corner — hover over the Choose a Backdrop button (it looks like a landscape photo icon).
  3. Click the magnifying glass icon to search the built-in library.
  4. Search for kitchen or blue sky or neon tunnel — pick anything that looks fun.
  5. Click on a backdrop to add it. It will instantly appear behind your cookie on the stage.
Paint your own backdrop Instead of searching, you can hover over the backdrop button and click the paintbrush icon to draw your own. A simple colour fill (use the paint bucket tool) is quick and effective — try a warm tan colour to match a bakery theme!
8
Test your game and add a click sound
Playing it for the first time and adding audio feedback

Testing

  1. Click the green flag above the stage. Your score should reset to 0.
  2. Click the cookie on the stage. The score should go up by 1 each time! The cookie should briefly switch to the "pressed" costume.
  3. If the score isn't going up, check that you selected your Cookie sprite in the sprite panel before writing the code in Step 5.

Adding a click sound (bonus!)

  1. Select the Cookie sprite in the sprite panel.
  2. Click the Sounds tab at the top.
  3. Hover over the speaker icon (bottom-left) and click the magnifying glass to search sounds.
  4. Search for "pop" and add it to your sprite.
  5. Go back to the Code tab. Click the Sound category (pink/purple blocks).
  6. Drag start sound Pop and snap it right after the when this sprite clicked block (before the costume switch).
Troubleshooting checklist Cookie not responding to clicks? Make sure: (1) you selected the Cookie sprite before coding, (2) you used "when this sprite clicked" not "when ⚑ clicked", (3) the green flag has been pressed to start the game.
✓
Your complete code — at a glance
Both scripts that should be on your Cookie sprite

Script 1: On green flag

when ⚑ clicked
set Score to 0
switch costume to normal

Script 2: On click

when this sprite clicked
start sound Pop
switch costume to pressed
change Score by 1
wait 0.1 seconds
switch costume to normal
